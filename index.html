Why Lanebreak exists

Swimming: the only sport you train blind.

After a run, you know your pace. After lifting, you know your reps. After cycling, you know your power. After a swim, you stare at distance, average pace, and calories - and still don't know:

  • Was today a good workout?
  • Did my endurance improve?
  • Why did I fall apart halfway through?
  • What should I do tomorrow?

Lanebreak was built to answer the question every swimmer asks walking out of the pool: "Did that actually help me get better?"

Why I built this

I wanted to improve my swims.

I swim regularly. I track everything. And yet - I constantly left the pool unsure if I improved. Some days felt amazing but the numbers were bad. Some days looked good on paper but felt awful. I realized: the problem wasn't my training. The problem was the feedback.

  • Existing tools were too expensive
  • Existing tools were too technical
  • Or they were dashboards with no coaching

So I built Lanebreak - first for myself, and now for swimmers like me.

What Lanebreak does

Lanebreak turns swim data into coaching.

Instead of raw stats, you get explanations.

Analyze Past Swims

Lanebreak breaks down your workout into:

    - pacing consistency
    - fatigue patterns
    - efficiency changes

You’ll know where the workout went right — and exactly where it fell apart.

Know What To Do Next

Based on your recent workouts and fatigue patterns, the app suggests whether you should:

    - push
    - hold steady
    - recover

After every session, Lanebreak suggests your next workout - not generic sets, but ones matched to what your body actually needs.
